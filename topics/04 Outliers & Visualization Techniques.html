Outliers and Tukey Boxplots

Tukey Boxplots are used to visualize the distribution of data and identify outliers:

  • Median: The middle data point after sorting.
  • Q1 (First Quartile): The middle data point below the median.
  • Q3 (Third Quartile): The middle data point above the median.
  • IQR (Interquartile Range): Calculated as Q3 - Q1.
  • Whiskers (Inner Fence):
    • Upper Limit: Q3 + 1.5 × IQR.
    • Lower Limit: Q1 - 1.5 × IQR.
    • Upper Inner Fence: Highest data point ≤ Upper Limit.
    • Lower Inner Fence: Lowest data point ≥ Lower Limit.
  • Suspected Outliers: Data points greater than 1.5 × IQR below Q1 or above Q3 (represented by circles).
  • Outliers: Data points greater than 3 × IQR below Q1 or above Q3 (represented by black dots).

Basic Visualization Techniques

Basic visualizations help communicate insights from data effectively. Common types include:

  • Line Plots: Used to show trends over time or a continuous variable.
  • Boxplots: Useful for visualizing the distribution of data and identifying outliers.
  • Histograms: Show the frequency distribution of a single variable, helping understand data spread.
  • Bar Charts: Used to compare categorical data.
  • Scatter Plots: Visualize the relationship between two numerical variables.
  • Heatmaps: Represent data values through variations in color, useful for correlation matrices.
  • Parallel Coordinates: Visualize multivariate data by showing each variable on a separate vertical axis.
