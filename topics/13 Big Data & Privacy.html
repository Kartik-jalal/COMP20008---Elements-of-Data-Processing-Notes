COMP20008 - Big Data & Privacy: Exam Notes

Privacy Loss Budget & Differential Privacy

Privacy Loss Budget (k): A parameter used to balance privacy protection with data utility in differential privacy.

  • High k: Larger privacy loss, higher utility.
  • Low k: Lower privacy loss, reduced utility.

Global Sensitivity: The maximum difference in query answers due to the presence or absence of an individual record.

Differential Privacy: A method to ensure that the output of a query does not reveal much about any one individual in the dataset.

Big Data Analytics & Ethical Considerations

Big Data Analytics: Techniques used to extract meaningful insights from large datasets.

  • Stakeholders: Individuals, organizations, and society affected by data collection and analysis.
  • Processes & Implications: The data processing flow must consider its ethical impact on stakeholders.

10 Simple Rules for Ethical Big Data Research:

  1. Acknowledge that data are people and can cause harm.
  2. Recognize that privacy is more than just data security.
  3. Guard against re-identification by linking datasets.
  4. Practice ethical data stewardship, even if the data is public.

Examples of Data Sources & Decision-Making Process

Examples of Data Sources:

  • TowerData - Data intelligence services.
  • IRI - Consumer and retail market data.
  • Intelius - People search services.

Decision-Making Process: Big data can be used to support decision-making processes by analyzing data quality, completeness, and its context.

  • Big Data Quality: Assessing accuracy and completeness for aggregated data.
  • Mosaic Effect: Combining different data sources can reveal information about individuals even if each source is anonymized.

10 Simple Rules for Responsible Big Data Research

Rule 1: Acknowledge that data are people and can do harm.

Rule 2: Recognize that privacy is more than just data security.

Rule 3: Understand that data can be re-identified by linking datasets.

Rule 4: Be aware of the biases present in datasets.

Rule 5: Consider the impact on people when using algorithms to process data.

For more detailed rules, refer to the source: PLOS Computational Biology.

When to Break Privacy Rules

Exceptions: In certain situations, it may be necessary to temporarily set aside privacy considerations:

  • Natural Disasters: To coordinate rescue efforts, sharing location data may be required.
  • Public Health Emergencies: Contact tracing during disease outbreaks may need data sharing.
  • Hostile Situations: Protecting individuals' safety may require breaking privacy rules.

However, such exceptions should be used sparingly and ethically.

Empowering Individuals

Empowering Individuals: It is essential to provide individuals with tools to understand and control their own data.

  • Education: Raising social awareness about data privacy and its implications.
  • Data Access & Control: Allowing individuals to access and manage their data, e.g., Google activity control settings.
