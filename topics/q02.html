COMP20008 - Classification Problems: Exam Practice

Question 2

Which of the following is the best example of a classification problem?

  • Predicting whether a customer will purchase an item at an online retailer based on their browsing history.
  • Predicting which sports team will win an upcoming match.
  • Predicting the likelihood that an Instagram user will like a given photo.
  • Predicting how much a customer will spend at an online retailer in the next year based on their past purchase history.

Correct Answer: Predicting whether a customer will purchase an item at an online retailer based on their browsing history.

Explanation: This is a classification problem because it involves predicting a categorical outcome (i.e., "purchase" or "not purchase"). Classification problems are characterized by predicting discrete labels or categories, such as whether something is true or false.

Practice Question 1

Which of the following is an example of a classification problem?

  • Predicting the price of a stock at the end of the day.
  • Predicting whether a given email is spam or not spam.
  • Predicting the temperature in a city tomorrow.
  • Predicting the number of units sold of a product next month.

Correct Answer: Predicting whether a given email is spam or not spam.

Explanation: This is a classification problem because it involves predicting a label (i.e., "spam" or "not spam"). Classification problems focus on assigning inputs to predefined categories.

Practice Question 2

Which of the following tasks would be best suited for classification analysis?

  • Determining the probability of rain tomorrow.
  • Predicting if a given bank transaction is fraudulent or not.
  • Predicting the height of a person based on age and gender.
  • Predicting the annual revenue of a company.

Correct Answer: Predicting if a given bank transaction is fraudulent or not.

Explanation: This is a classification problem because it involves assigning the label "fraudulent" or "not fraudulent" to each transaction. The goal is to classify transactions into one of these two categories.

Practice Question 3

Which of the following is not an example of a classification problem?

  • Predicting whether a person has diabetes based on their health data.
  • Classifying emails into different folders based on their content.
  • Predicting the price of a house based on its features.
  • Determining whether a customer will renew their subscription or not.

Correct Answer: Predicting the price of a house based on its features.

Explanation: Predicting the price of a house is a regression problem, as it involves estimating a continuous numerical value. Classification problems, on the other hand, involve assigning categories or labels.

Summary

Classification problems involve predicting a label or category for an input, such as whether an email is spam or not, or whether a customer will purchase a product. These problems differ from regression problems, which predict continuous numerical values. Understanding the nature of the output helps determine whether a classification or regression model is appropriate for a given problem.
