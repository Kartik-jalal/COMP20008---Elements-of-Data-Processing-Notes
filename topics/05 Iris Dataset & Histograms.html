COMP20008 - Iris Dataset & Histograms: Exam Notes

Iris Dataset Overview

The Iris dataset is a well-known dataset introduced by statistician Ronald Fisher. It contains 150 samples of iris flowers, each with four features:

  • Petal Width
  • Petal Length
  • Sepal Width
  • Sepal Length

There are three species of iris flowers represented in the dataset:

  • Setosa
  • Virginica
  • Versicolour

For more details, refer to the Wikipedia page on the Iris dataset.

Histograms

Histograms are used to represent the frequency distribution of data points, such as the petal width of iris flowers. Important points to remember about histograms include:

  • The appearance of a histogram can vary significantly depending on the bin size chosen.
  • Smaller bin sizes can provide more detail, while larger bins offer a more general overview of the data.

Outliers

Outliers are data points that deviate significantly from the rest of the dataset. Identifying and analyzing outliers can provide insights or indicate errors. Example:

  • Paternity Case Study: Outliers have been used in practical scenarios, such as determining paternity. Reference: V. Barnett, "The study of outliers," Journal of the Royal Statistical Society, 1978.

Bar Charts

Bar charts are used to summarize data points across different categories. They are effective in visualizing categorical data, such as the frequency of flower species in the Iris dataset. Key characteristics include:

  • Used for comparing discrete categories.
  • Can be used to represent both counts and summary statistics.
